Accidents in North American Climbing

This is a visual look at the causes of accidents in North American climbing, and how each cause contributes to the total number of accidents. Causes in blue are "Immediate" causes, and causes in orange are "Contributing" causes. Please note that while both data for the time period of 1959 to 2014 and 2015 alone can be viewed, comparisons between the two should not be made as the totals are significantly smaller for 2015. This is also the case for USA vs Canada. The purpose is to see how the causes of accidents compare to each other.

The data used is from the 2016 edition of Accidents in North American Climbing published by the American Alpine Club.
